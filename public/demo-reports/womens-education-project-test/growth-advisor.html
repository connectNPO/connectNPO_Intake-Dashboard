1. Overall Overview

Women's Education Project has built something durable. The mission line — Her Leadership, Her Livelihood — is distinctive and human. The Leadership Academy / LAMP model and the Collaboration Lab give the program clear shape. Governance, India-based leadership, financials going back to 2012, annual updates through 2025, and student/alumnae stories are all visible on the public site. These are the assets most nonprofits at this stage are still trying to assemble.

The diagnosis is that the growth question for WEP is not whether the mission story is strong enough. It is whether the supporter pathways around that story are reliable, packaged, and easy to act on. From the public review, two soft spots stand out: a footer Get Involved link that returned a 404 during the test crawl, and homepage impact counters that rendered as 0 even though body copy cites 4,000+ alumnae. Both are reversible, but each one quietly costs trust on first visit. The recommended primary focus for the next 30 days is supporter-pathway repair plus donor packaging; broader SEO expansion and a redesign should wait.

The rest of this report walks the fourteen intake categories in the order the intake form asked them, then closes with an overall result and a focus/plan section.

16. Overall Result & Practical Plan

Overall result

WEP is not held back by mission, programs, or trust artifacts — those are real strengths and should not be touched in the next 90 days. The actual constraint is supporter-pathway reliability and packaging: a broken Get Involved link, homepage counters reading 0, and a donate page with warm voice but no concrete gift impact. Each of these quietly costs trust on a first visit. The good news is that they are small, reversible fixes — and fixing them first compounds the value of every later move (more SEO, more press, more story embeds, more grant applications). Broader SEO expansion, paid acquisition, and a full redesign should wait until the front porch is repaired.

What to focus on first

These are the highest-leverage repairs and should land inside the next 30 days:

  • Current Challenges (§6) and Website & Digital Presence (§7). Fix the /get-involved/ 404 and stabilize the homepage impact counters. Lock the canonical one-sentence definition across the homepage <title>, meta description, hero, and social previews.
  • Donor & Supporter Readiness (§8). Once gift-impact and Circle of Light ranges are confirmed internally, publish a short gift-impact block and a monthly-giving value proposition on /donate/.
  • Volunteer Readiness (§9), starting after §6 is done. Build out three to five named volunteer pathways on the restored Get Involved page, with one named internal owner per pathway.

What can wait

These are real but not yet ripe. Picking them up before the items above just creates churn:

  • Programs & Services (§4). No new program is needed. Tighten the one-sentence program summary (§11) and reuse it everywhere — that does most of the work a new program write-up would do.
  • Grant Readiness (§13). Once §10 (outcome context note) and §11 (locked program summary) are done, the grant packaging mostly assembles itself. No separate grant content stream is needed in the first 30 days.
  • Operations & Automation (§14), beyond the channel-ownership map. Ship the ownership map first so each future automation has a defined manual step to replace. Defer any new tool purchases until ownership is decided.
  • SEO expansion, paid acquisition, full redesign. All three amplify whatever state the site is in. Spend on amplification after the visible breakage is repaired.

Why this order matters

Each step makes the next one cheaper, and each step skipped makes the later steps more expensive:

  1. Fix the broken pathway first so later traffic doesn't bounce off a 404 or "0 alumnae" counter.
  2. Package gift impact next so the donors who are arriving have a concrete reason to give today instead of "maybe later."
  3. Build out volunteer pathways third, because by then there is a working page to point them to.
  4. Frame trust and methodology context fourth, since those edits compound on top of a site that already looks reliable.
  5. Tune SEO and AI-search signals last, so amplification lands on a site that earns the visit.

Reversing this order — for example, running ads before the Get Involved page is fixed — typically wastes the spend twice: once on the click that bounces, and once on the brand impression that says "this org isn't quite together."
