## 1. Overall Overview

Ayana Foundation, on the strength of its public site, already does the hardest part well. The hero promise ("Elevating Women Who Build to Scale"), the three-pillar model (Founder Development, Trusted Community, Enhanced Funding Access), the 12-week Raise Accelerator, the public partners and board, the impact numbers, and the educational-only footer disclaimer are all visible and consistent. That is the foundation most early-stage nonprofits are still trying to assemble.

The diagnosis is that Ayana is not held back by missing content or a weak brand. The real constraint is **pathway architecture** — the work of deciding, per audience (founder, donor, sponsor, mentor, partner), what the next click should be and what each audience needs to see before they take it. Donor packaging, impact-metric methodology, and follow-up clarity all sit downstream of that one decision.

The recommended primary focus for the next 30 days is to clarify the founder vs. donor vs. mentor pathways on the homepage and donate page. SEO/AI-search tuning, resource-library reorganization, and operations mapping are real wins but should wait until the pathway work is in place. The rest of this report walks the fourteen intake categories in the order the intake form asked them, then closes with an overall result and a focus/plan section.

### Homepage title and meta description use internal language

- **Evidence / Source URL:** https://ayana.org/

- **Recommendation:** Rewrite the title and meta description in plain founder/donor language. ### Resource library is rich in long-tail founder topics

## 16. Overall Result & Practical Plan

### Overall result

Ayana is not held back by content or brand. The current constraint is **pathway architecture** — turning a strong public site into clearer per-audience next steps for founders, donors/sponsors, and mentors/partners. Three small edits (pathway clarity, donor packaging, impact methodology) unlock most of the value that is already on the site. Broader SEO, redesign, and platform changes should wait.

### Where to focus first / what can wait

- **Focus first — Website & Digital Presence (§7).** Decide the audience-pick band and reconcile the "Founder Development" nav label with its destination.
- **Focus first — Donor & Supporter Readiness (§8).** Package gift impact on the homepage and donate page with internally confirmed figures.
- **Focus first — Trust & Transparency Signals (§10).** Footnote the impact numbers with date range, definitions, and source.
- **Can wait — Programs & Services (§4).** No new program is needed; existing description is strong.
- **Can wait — Operations & Automation (§14, beyond the ownership map).** Build the form-ownership map first; defer tool purchases until ownership is decided.
- **Can wait — Grant Readiness (§13).** Reuse the impact methodology once it ships; do not add a separate grant content stream yet.

### 30-day direction

1. **Clarify the "Founder Development" top-nav label and link.**
   *(From: §7 Website & Digital Presence.)*
   Decide whether the nav item should send visitors to `/programs` or to a resources page, then make the label match the destination.
   **Done when:** the top-nav label and the page it loads tell the same story, and an internal reviewer can describe in one sentence what that nav item does.

2. **Add a "What your gift funds" block near Donate Now.**
   *(From: §8 Donor & Supporter Readiness.)*
   Two to three gift-impact tiers, written in plain language, placed on the homepage and on `/fundraising`. Keep the educational-only disclaimer visible.
   **Done when:** the block is live on both pages, the figures have been confirmed by whoever approves donor copy, and the same language appears on the Givebutter page.

3. **Footnote the impact numbers with a date range and definitions link.**
   *(From: §10 Trust & Transparency Signals.)*
   Add one small line under the impact row pointing at a short methodology section. Confirm the underlying numbers first; do not publish numbers that have not been re-checked.
   **Done when:** every public impact number on the homepage has a date and a defined source on a methodology page.

### 90-day direction

1. **Ship the audience-pick band on the homepage.**
   *(From: §7 Website & Digital Presence.)*
   Three tiles (founder / donor or sponsor / mentor or partner), each ending in a specific verb that matches the destination.
   **Done when:** the band is live, each tile loads the right destination, and analytics (if available) can distinguish clicks by audience.

2. **Add expectations and fit copy to `/apply`.**
   *(From: §4 Programs & Services and §7 Website & Digital Presence.)*
   A "Who this is for" block and a "What happens after you apply" block, sourced from internally confirmed cadence and definitions.
   **Done when:** both blocks are live on `/apply`, cross-linked from `/programs`, and reflect the actual program-fit and review timeline.

3. **Reorganize the resource library into clear categories with internal links.**
   *(From: §7 Website & Digital Presence.)*
   Group resources into a small number of categories (Fundraising, Operations & Legal, Brand, Product/AI) on the index page; add internal links from each resource detail page to the relevant program touchpoint.
   **Done when:** the index page groups every resource, each detail page links to a sibling resource or program page, and `<title>` / meta description / `<h1>` are consistent across the library.

4. **Publish a community stewardship panel and a labeled mentor/partner path.**
   *(From: §9 Volunteer Readiness.)*
   A short panel at the top of `/community` describing how the space works, plus a labeled mentor/partner path distinct from donor and applicant paths.
   **Done when:** both are live, each public form has a visible response promise, and an internal form-ownership map exists (kept private — see §14).

5. **Tighten title, meta description, and social cards.**
   *(From: §7 Website & Digital Presence.)*
   Replace internal-sounding language with founder/donor-readable wording. Make the same canonical sentence appear in the homepage `<title>`, meta description, social card, and the About page opener so AI summaries converge.
   **Done when:** the four surfaces use the same canonical sentence and a quick browser preview check matches across them.
