## 2. What This Area Evaluates

Messaging & Positioning evaluates whether the nonprofit has public-facing information and internal readiness signals that help outside audiences understand the work and take the next step.

A strong foundation should make these things clear:

- A first-time visitor can understand who the nonprofit serves within a few seconds.
- Program language is plain and audience-focused.
- The main message connects mission to action.
- The message explains problem, audience, activity, and benefit.
- Different pages use consistent language.
- The primary CTA fits the message.

This area is an operational readiness review only. It should help connect intake answers, public evidence, expert standards, and practical next steps without overclaiming.

## 3. Intake Signals to Use

Relevant intake fields:

```text
- Field/key: message_clarity_status
  Why it matters: Shows how the nonprofit currently sees its message clarity.
  Strong signal: Specific clarity issue or confidence level is identified.
  Weak or missing signal: “Not sure” or broad concern without examples.

- Field/key: primary_goal
  Why it matters: Messaging should support the most important near-term outcome.
  Strong signal: Clear donor, volunteer, funder, partner, or service-user priority.
  Weak or missing signal: No clear priority or too many equal priorities.

- Field/key: current_programs_or_services
  Why it matters: Messaging must explain programs in plain public language.
  Strong signal: Programs can be described by audience, need, activity, and outcome.
  Weak or missing signal: Internal program names without explanation.

- Field/key: stuck_points
  Why it matters: Shows what confusion may be blocking action.
  Strong signal: Specific problem such as visitors not taking action or programs being hard to explain.
  Weak or missing signal: No connection between message and practical blocker.

- Field/key: priority_audience_90_days
  Why it matters: The main message should speak to the audience that matters most now.
  Strong signal: Audience is clear, such as donors, volunteers, funders, partners, or participants.
  Weak or missing signal: Audience is unclear or everyone is the priority.
```

Rules:

- Do not treat intake answers as proven public facts.
- Use intake to understand goals, constraints, and stated concerns.
- Confirm outward-facing claims with public evidence when possible.
- If a signal is missing or private, mark it as `Needs confirmation`.

Evidence item: Navigation labels

What counts as strong evidence: Labels match visitor tasks.

Evidence rules:

- If evidence is not visible publicly, label it `Not found` or `Needs confirmation`.

## 5. Strong Signals

- A first-time visitor can understand who the nonprofit serves within a few seconds.
- Program language is plain and audience-focused.
- The main message connects mission to action.
- The message explains problem, audience, activity, and benefit.
- Different pages use consistent language.
- The primary CTA fits the message.
- Program names are supported by plain explanations.
- The message is specific enough for donors, volunteers, funders, and partners.

---

## 6. Warning Signs

- Mission language is broad but not specific.
- Program names are internal or unclear.
- The homepage does not say who is served.
- The organization explains values but not practical work.
- Different pages describe the work in different ways.
- CTA language does not match the stated priority.
- Visitors must already know the organization to understand it.

Rules:

- Use supportive language.
- Avoid blame or audit-like phrasing.
- Avoid legal, tax, accounting, HR, or compliance determinations.
- Phrase findings as readiness improvement opportunities.

## 7. Recommended Analysis Pattern

Use this structure:

```text
1. Start with the nonprofit's stated concern or goal.
2. Compare intake signals with public evidence.
3. Identify the main readiness gap.
4. Explain why the gap matters for donors, volunteers, funders, partners, staff, or community members.
5. Recommend one practical 30-day improvement.
6. Recommend one 90-day improvement if capacity allows.
7. Mark uncertain items as “Needs confirmation.”
```

## 8. Common Recommendations

### Recommendation 1: Rewrite the homepage message in plain language

```text
When to use: Homepage hero is vague or slogan-only.
Evidence required: Homepage hero + About page review.
30-day version: Add one sentence stating who is served, what is provided, and why it matters.
90-day version: Align homepage, About, program, and donation/volunteer language around the same core message.
Effort level: Low
Expected benefit: Helps new visitors understand the organization faster.
Risk if ignored: Visitors may leave before understanding the work.
```

### Recommendation 2: Add plain-language program explanations

```text
When to use: Program names are internal or unclear.
Evidence required: Program page and navigation review.
30-day version: Add a one-sentence explanation under each program name.
90-day version: Create a program overview that maps audience, need, activity, and outcome.
Effort level: Medium
Expected benefit: Makes programs easier to understand and support.
Risk if ignored: Important work may seem vague or inaccessible.
```

### Recommendation 3: Clarify the priority audience

```text
When to use: Messaging tries to speak to everyone equally.
Evidence required: Homepage, intake priority, and CTA review.
30-day version: Add copy that addresses the current priority audience directly.
90-day version: Create audience-specific pathways for donors, volunteers, funders, partners, or participants.
Effort level: Medium
Expected benefit: Makes the next step more relevant.
Risk if ignored: No audience feels clearly guided.
```

### Recommendation 4: Connect mission to action

```text
When to use: Mission is clear but next step is not.
Evidence required: Hero, CTA, and relevant landing page review.
30-day version: Add a short bridge sentence before the primary CTA.
90-day version: Build a message-to-action pathway across key pages.
Effort level: Low to Medium
Expected benefit: Helps visitors know how to respond.
Risk if ignored: Visitors may agree with the mission but not act.
```

### Recommendation 5: Create a simple message guide

```text
When to use: Different pages or team descriptions are inconsistent.
Evidence required: Website copy + intake note review.
30-day version: Draft a short internal message guide with core description, audience, and approved phrases.
90-day version: Use the guide to refresh website, donor, volunteer, and funder language.
Effort level: Medium
Expected benefit: Improves consistency across communications.
Risk if ignored: Messaging stays fragmented.
```

## 9. What NOT to Say

Do not say:

- “Your messaging is bad.”
- “No one understands you.”
- “This will increase donations.”
- “You need a full rebrand.”
- “Your mission is wrong.”

Safer alternatives:

- “The public message could be clearer for first-time visitors.”
- “A plain-language program explanation may help visitors understand the work faster.”
- “This is a messaging readiness observation, not a branding judgment.”

---

## 10. Safety, Privacy, and Disclaimer Rules

Use these rules:

- Do not request passwords, donor lists, employee records, private client/member data, bank information, private financial statements, confidential grant files, or sensitive internal documents.
- Do not provide legal, tax, accounting, HR, insurance, grant eligibility, or compliance advice.
- Do not promise fundraising, ranking, grant, volunteer, or operational outcomes.
- Distinguish public evidence from intake answers and assumptions.
- Label uncertain items as `Needs confirmation`.
- Draft for human review before customer delivery.
- Keep recommendations operational and public-facing unless a later secure workflow is explicitly built.

If this area overlaps with **Financial & Compliance Readiness**, the related client-facing section must include:

> This section is an operational readiness review, not accounting, tax, or legal advice.

```text

Why it matters: If the material is safe and intended to be public, making it easier to find could strengthen readiness.

Recommended next step: Confirm what can be shared publicly, then add a short summary or link.
```
