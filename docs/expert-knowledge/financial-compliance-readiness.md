## 2. What This Area Evaluates

Financial & Compliance Readiness evaluates whether the nonprofit has public-facing information and internal readiness signals that help outside audiences understand the work and take the next step.

A strong foundation should make these things clear:

- Public-facing financial or accountability materials are easy to find.
- Organization can explain how funding supports programs.
- Grant reporting readiness is described operationally.
- Annual/impact materials connect resources to program outcomes.
- Public profiles or reports are linked when appropriate.
- Sensitive details are not exposed.

This area is an operational readiness review only. It should help connect intake answers, public evidence, expert standards, and practical next steps without overclaiming.

## 3. Intake Signals to Use

Relevant intake fields:

```text
- Field/key: public_financial_compliance_materials
  Why it matters: Shows what public-facing materials may already exist.
  Strong signal: Public Form 990/profile link, annual report, financial summary, grant reporting process, or budget process is identified.
  Weak or missing signal: “Not sure” or no materials ready.

- Field/key: financial_compliance_challenge
  Why it matters: Shows what feels hardest operationally.
  Strong signal: Specific challenge like explaining finances to funders or tracking program costs.
  Weak or missing signal: Vague concern or professional support needed without details.

- Field/key: grant_related_materials
  Why it matters: Financial readiness often supports grant readiness.
  Strong signal: Budget/reporting process or program expense tracking is described.
  Weak or missing signal: No operational reporting material identified.

- Field/key: public_trust_materials
  Why it matters: Public accountability materials support donor and funder trust.
  Strong signal: Annual report, public profile, or financial summary is available.
  Weak or missing signal: Materials are missing or hard to find.

- Field/key: implementation_capacity_30_days
  Why it matters: Keeps recommendations safe and realistic.
  Strong signal: Capacity for organizing links, public summaries, or confirmation with a professional.
  Weak or missing signal: Capacity unknown.
```

Rules:

- Do not treat intake answers as proven public facts.
- Use intake to understand goals, constraints, and stated concerns.
- Confirm outward-facing claims with public evidence when possible.
- If a signal is missing or private, mark it as `Needs confirmation`.

## 4. Public Evidence to Collect

Primary evidence:

- Public annual report.
- Public financial summary.
- Public Form 990/profile link.
- Grant/reporting language.
- Impact/financial connection.

For this area, collect:

```text
Evidence item: Public annual report
Where to look: Impact, transparency, About, footer, or reports pages.
What counts as strong evidence: Report is findable and explains funding/program connection.
What counts as weak/missing evidence: Report missing, outdated, or buried.
Suggested citation format: Annual report, [URL]

Evidence item: Public financial summary
Where to look: Transparency/accountability page or annual report.
What counts as strong evidence: Summary is plain-language and clearly public.
What counts as weak/missing evidence: No public summary or unclear context.
Suggested citation format: Public financial summary, [URL]

Evidence item: Public Form 990/profile link
Where to look: Transparency page, footer, public nonprofit profile link.
What counts as strong evidence: Linked as a public transparency resource.
What counts as weak/missing evidence: Not linked or hard to find.
Suggested citation format: Public profile/Form 990 link, [URL]

Evidence item: Grant/reporting language
Where to look: Grant/funder/impact/report pages.
What counts as strong evidence: Operational reporting readiness is described without sensitive detail.
What counts as weak/missing evidence: No reporting or budget process language.
Suggested citation format: Grant/reporting language, [URL]

Evidence item: Impact/financial connection
Where to look: Annual report, impact page, donation page.
What counts as strong evidence: Public materials explain how funding supports programs.
What counts as weak/missing evidence: Financial information is disconnected from mission impact.
Suggested citation format: Funding-to-impact language, [URL]
```

Evidence rules:

- Prefer public materials linked from the nonprofit's own website.
- Do not request or store sensitive/private documents.
- If evidence is not visible publicly, label it `Not found` or `Needs confirmation`.
- Do not infer private facts from public silence.

## 5. Strong Signals

- Public-facing financial or accountability materials are easy to find.
- Organization can explain how funding supports programs.
- Grant reporting readiness is described operationally.
- Annual/impact materials connect resources to program outcomes.
- Public profiles or reports are linked when appropriate.
- Sensitive details are not exposed.
- Professional-review boundaries are clear.
- Questions needing CPA/legal support are identified as such.

---

## 6. Warning Signs

- Public accountability materials are missing or hard to find.
- Program cost/impact connection is unclear.
- The organization indicates it needs professional accounting/tax support.
- Financial language is too detailed for public review or too vague to be useful.
- Public reports are outdated.
- Compliance-sensitive questions are mixed with general readiness recommendations.
- Private materials would be needed before any safe conclusion.

Rules:

- Use supportive language.
- Avoid blame or audit-like phrasing.
- Avoid legal, tax, accounting, HR, or compliance determinations.
- Phrase findings as readiness improvement opportunities.

## 7. Recommended Analysis Pattern

Use this structure:

```text
1. Start with the nonprofit's stated concern or goal.
2. Compare intake signals with public evidence.
3. Identify the main readiness gap.
4. Explain why the gap matters for donors, volunteers, funders, partners, staff, or community members.
5. Recommend one practical 30-day improvement.
6. Recommend one 90-day improvement if capacity allows.
7. Mark uncertain items as “Needs confirmation.”
```

## 8. Common Recommendations

### Recommendation 1: Add the required disclaimer and human-review note

```text
When to use: Any financial/compliance readiness section appears in a report.
Evidence required: Report section presence.
30-day version: Start the section with the required disclaimer and mark professional questions for review.
90-day version: Create a standard review workflow for CPA/legal-sensitive findings.
Effort level: Low
Expected benefit: Keeps the report safely within operational readiness.
Risk if ignored: Readers may mistake readiness observations for professional advice.
```

### Recommendation 2: Organize public accountability links

```text
When to use: Public profile, Form 990, annual report, or summaries exist but are hard to find.
Evidence required: Website transparency/footer/report review.
30-day version: Add a public accountability link group where appropriate.
90-day version: Create a transparency page with reports, profile links, impact, and contact path.
Effort level: Medium
Expected benefit: Improves donor and funder confidence.
Risk if ignored: Useful materials stay buried.
```

### Recommendation 3: Explain funding-to-program connection

```text
When to use: Public materials do not show how funding supports programs.
Evidence required: Donate, annual report, program, impact page review.
30-day version: Add a plain-language note connecting funding to program activities or outcomes.
90-day version: Build a funding-and-impact summary by program.
Effort level: Medium
Expected benefit: Helps donors and funders understand resource use at a high level.
Risk if ignored: Financial information may feel disconnected from mission.
```

### Recommendation 4: Mark professional support needs clearly

```text
When to use: Intake indicates accounting/tax/legal uncertainty.
Evidence required: Intake challenge + public evidence.
30-day version: Label the item as needing qualified professional review rather than giving advice.
90-day version: Create an internal checklist of items to ask a CPA/legal advisor.
Effort level: Low
Expected benefit: Avoids unsafe recommendations.
Risk if ignored: The report may overstep expertise.
```

### Recommendation 5: Create a basic public financial summary only if safe

```text
When to use: The organization wants public-facing financial clarity but has no simple summary.
Evidence required: Intake + public reports/profile review.
30-day version: Draft a high-level summary for human/professional review before publishing.
90-day version: Create an annual public accountability summary connected to impact.
Effort level: Medium to High
Expected benefit: Supports trust without exposing private details.
Risk if ignored: Publishing without review could expose sensitive or incorrect information.
```

## 9. What NOT to Say

Do not say:

- “You are compliant/noncompliant.”
- “You should file Form 990 this way.”
- “Your accounting is wrong.”
- “Your board-approved budget process is adequate.”
- “You should publish private financial statements.”
- “This is tax/legal/accounting advice.”

Safer alternatives:

- “This section is an operational readiness review, not accounting, tax, or legal advice.”
- “This item should be reviewed by a qualified professional.”
- “Public accountability materials could be easier to find.”

---

## 10. Safety, Privacy, and Disclaimer Rules

Use these rules:

- Do not request passwords, donor lists, employee records, private client/member data, bank information, private financial statements, confidential grant files, or sensitive internal documents.
- Do not provide legal, tax, accounting, HR, insurance, grant eligibility, or compliance advice.
- Do not promise fundraising, ranking, grant, volunteer, or operational outcomes.
- Distinguish public evidence from intake answers and assumptions.
- Label uncertain items as `Needs confirmation`.
- Draft for human review before customer delivery.
- The client-facing section must begin with exactly: “This section is an operational readiness review, not accounting, tax, or legal advice.”

If this area overlaps with **Financial & Compliance Readiness**, the related client-facing section must include:

> This section is an operational readiness review, not accounting, tax, or legal advice.

```text

Why it matters: If this area is a priority, visitors may not know how to understand or act on the information.

Recommended next step: Add a short plain-language section that explains the current status and next step.
```

### Needs Confirmation Example

```text
What we saw: The intake notes mention this material, but we could not confirm a public version on the website.

Why it matters: If the material is safe and intended to be public, making it easier to find could strengthen readiness.

Recommended next step: Confirm what can be shared publicly, then add a short summary or link.
```
