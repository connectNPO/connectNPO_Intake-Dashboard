# Strategy Diagnosis Framework v1

**Owner:** connectNPO operations  
**Status:** Draft v1  
**Use:** Consulting brain for Growth Readiness Reports. This framework helps the report team move beyond section-by-section observations and decide what a nonprofit should focus on next.

---

## 1. Purpose

The Growth Readiness Report should act like a practical advisor, not a generic audit.

The goal is to help each nonprofit understand:

- Where they are strong today.
- Where they are blocked.
- Which opportunities matter most right now.
- What to improve first.
- What can wait.
- What needs professional review.
- How to move forward over the next 30 and 90 days.

This framework should be used after intake review and public evidence collection, and before the final report is written.

---

## 2. Core Principle

Do not give every organization the same advice.

Each nonprofit has a different mix of:

- Mission clarity.
- Program maturity.
- Donor readiness.
- Grant readiness.
- Website quality.
- Content strength.
- Operational capacity.
- Financial/accountability readiness.
- Staff or volunteer bandwidth.
- Urgency and current goals.

A strong report should identify the best next direction for that specific organization.

---

## 3. Required Inputs

Before diagnosis, collect these inputs when available:

```text
1. Intake answers
2. Public website evidence
3. Public nonprofit profile / accountability evidence when safely available
4. Expert knowledge files from docs/expert-knowledge/
5. Admin notes or human observations
6. Missing information list
```

Do not invent missing facts. If evidence is unavailable, mark it as `Missing`, `Not found`, or `Needs confirmation`.

---

## 4. Main Diagnosis Questions

The diagnosis should answer these questions:

```text
1. What is this organization trying to accomplish right now?
2. What appears to be working well?
3. What is blocking growth, trust, funding, engagement, or efficiency?
4. Which area would create the most useful improvement if addressed first?
5. Which recommendations are realistic within 30 days?
6. Which improvements require a 90-day plan?
7. Which items should be reviewed by a human professional?
```

---

## 5. Growth Readiness Areas

Use these areas when diagnosing the organization:

```text
- Website Clarity
- Messaging & Positioning
- Donor Conversion
- Volunteer Readiness
- Trust & Transparency
- SEO / GEO
- Content Strategy
- Grant Readiness
- Program Impact Readiness
- Financial & Compliance Readiness
- Operations & Automation Readiness
```

Each area should be evaluated using its expert knowledge file, intake signals, and public evidence.

---

## 6. Strength / Weakness / Priority Model

For each area, classify the current state:

```text
Strength
- The organization already has usable assets, clear signals, or strong public evidence.

Developing
- The area has some useful pieces but needs organization, clarity, or consistency.

Gap
- Important evidence, process, page, message, or path is missing or unclear.

Risk / Needs Review
- The area may involve legal, tax, accounting, compliance, privacy, security, or professional judgment.
```

Then assign a priority:

```text
High Priority
- Blocks donors, volunteers, funders, partners, or staff from taking action.
- Directly supports the organization’s stated current goal.
- Can unlock multiple other improvements.

Medium Priority
- Useful improvement, but not the main blocker.
- Helps trust, clarity, or operations but can follow the main priority.

Low Priority
- Nice to improve later.
- Not urgent based on current goals, capacity, or evidence.

Needs Confirmation
- Not enough evidence to diagnose safely.
```

---

## 7. Priority Decision Rules

Use these rules to decide what should come first.

### Rule 1 — If people cannot understand the organization, fix clarity first.

Prioritize Website Clarity / Messaging & Positioning when:

```text
- Homepage does not clearly say who is served, what the organization does, and what to do next.
- Program descriptions are vague or hard to find.
- Intake goals do not match public website messaging.
- The organization has good work but unclear public explanation.
```

Recommended focus:

```text
30 days: homepage headline, short mission explanation, program summary, primary CTA.
90 days: full messaging refresh, audience-specific pages, stronger impact storytelling.
```

### Rule 2 — If donations are a goal but the giving path is weak, prioritize donor conversion.

Prioritize Donor Conversion when:

```text
- Donor growth is a stated goal.
- Donate/support action is hard to find.
- Gift impact is not explained.
- Trust signals are missing near the donation ask.
- Donation path exists but lacks motivation or context.
```

Recommended focus:

```text
30 days: visible donate CTA, gift impact copy, trust signals near ask.
90 days: campaign page, donor follow-up sequence, donor content calendar.
```

### Rule 3 — If the organization wants grants, check readiness before recommending grant outreach.

Prioritize Grant Readiness / Program Impact / Financial Readiness when:

```text
- Grants are a current goal.
- Programs are active but outcomes are not clearly described.
- Public accountability materials are missing or hard to find.
- Budget/reporting readiness is unclear.
- Funder-facing language is weak.
```

Recommended focus:

```text
30 days: program one-pager, impact summary, public accountability links, grant materials inventory.
90 days: grant prospect list, outcomes framework, reporting workflow, funder-ready narrative.
```

Do not promise grant success. Do not provide legal, tax, or accounting advice.

### Rule 4 — If staff capacity is the blocker, prioritize automation before more marketing.

Prioritize Operations & Automation when:

```text
- Intake shows repetitive manual work.
- Follow-up is delayed or inconsistent.
- Staff are overwhelmed.
- Data is scattered across forms, email, spreadsheets, or documents.
- The organization cannot handle more inquiries without better systems.
```

Recommended focus:

```text
30 days: map one workflow, automate one form-to-email/spreadsheet process, create follow-up templates.
90 days: CRM/light database, intake dashboard, automated reminders, reporting dashboard.
```

### Rule 5 — If the website is clear but discoverability is weak, prioritize SEO / GEO and content.

Prioritize SEO / GEO / Content Strategy when:

```text
- The organization is understandable but hard to find online.
- Page titles, headings, and service descriptions are weak.
- There is little educational or community content.
- The site lacks FAQ-style answers that search engines and AI systems can understand.
- Local or issue-specific search visibility matters.
```

Recommended focus:

```text
30 days: page titles, headings, meta descriptions, FAQ block, service-area language.
90 days: content calendar, issue guides, program pages, schema opportunities, AI-search friendly summaries.
```

### Rule 6 — If trust evidence is missing, strengthen transparency before aggressive fundraising.

Prioritize Trust & Transparency when:

```text
- Donor/funder action is requested but leadership, impact, reports, or contact information are unclear.
- Public accountability materials are missing or outdated.
- Testimonials, stories, outcomes, or proof points are hard to find.
- The organization asks for support but does not provide enough confidence signals.
```

Recommended focus:

```text
30 days: contact clarity, leadership/team basics, impact highlights, public links.
90 days: annual report page, transparency page, story library, donor/funder proof package.
```

### Rule 7 — If compliance or financial issues appear, stay in operational-readiness language.

Prioritize Financial & Compliance Readiness only within safe boundaries when:

```text
- Public financial or accountability materials are missing.
- The organization is unsure what can be shared publicly.
- Grant/funder readiness depends on clearer budget/reporting materials.
- Intake raises legal, tax, accounting, registration, or compliance concerns.
```

Recommended focus:

```text
30 days: organize public links, identify missing materials, prepare questions for CPA/legal review.
90 days: plain-language financial summary, reporting calendar, grant budget support workflow.
```

Always include a human-review note. Do not give legal, tax, accounting, or compliance advice.

## 9. How to Sound Like a Consultant

Use language that is specific, evidence-based, and directional.

Prefer:

```text
Based on the intake goal and the public website review, the most useful near-term focus appears to be donor trust and gift clarity, not a full website redesign.
```

Avoid:

```text
Your website needs improvement.
```

Prefer:

```text
The organization has a strong program foundation, but the website does not yet translate that strength into funder-ready evidence. A 90-day focus on program outcomes, impact language, and public accountability materials would likely create more value than general marketing activity.
```

Avoid:

```text
You should apply for more grants.
```

Prefer:

```text
Before expanding grant outreach, the organization should prepare a clearer program summary, outcomes evidence, and a basic grant materials inventory. This will make future grant research and applications more efficient.
```

---

## 10. What NOT to Do

Do not:

- Give the same recommendations to every nonprofit.
- Recommend marketing before the organization can handle follow-up.
- Recommend grant outreach before basic readiness materials exist.
- Recommend SEO/GEO before core messaging is understandable.
- Claim donor behavior, grant eligibility, or compliance status without evidence.
- Invent website findings that were not observed.
- Give legal, tax, accounting, or compliance advice.
- Overwhelm the organization with too many priorities.

---

## 11. Final Report Guidance

The final report should make the nonprofit feel guided, not judged.

Each report should clearly answer:

```text
Where are we now?
What is already working?
What is holding us back?
What should we focus on first?
What can wait?
What should we prepare next?
What support might we need?
```

The final recommendation should usually include:

```text
- 1 primary strategic focus
- 2 supporting priorities
- 3 practical 30-day actions
- 3 broader 90-day directions
- clear evidence labels
- human review / professional review flags when needed
```

---

## 12. Completion Criteria

A diagnosis is complete when:

```text
- It uses intake and public evidence.
- It references the relevant expert knowledge areas.
- It identifies strengths as well as gaps.
- It prioritizes what matters most now.
- It avoids unsafe professional advice.
- It gives realistic 30-day and 90-day direction.
- It leaves unknowns clearly marked as missing or needs confirmation.
```
